Ushers Ministry

Who is an Usher?

An usher is a church representative who ensures a warm and welcoming environment for all parishioners and visitors. Ushers play a crucial role in organizing the congregation during Mass by directing them to their seats, assisting with collections, and ensuring orderly movement, especially during Holy Communion. They are also responsible for distributing church materials such as bulletins and ensuring the overall smooth operation of the service.

Ushers must possess good interpersonal skills, have a welcoming attitude, and be capable of managing crowds calmly and efficiently. They are the first point of contact for parishioners, ensuring that everyone feels valued and comfortable during their time at the church.

The Ushers Ministry

The Ushers Ministry unites all ushers who serve at St. Cyprian Kawanda Parish. It was established with a goal of providing more structured guidance for ushers' roles and responsibilities. The ministry ensures that all ushers adhere to a high standard of service, professionalism, and conduct during their duties.

The leadership team is composed of a chairperson, vice chairperson, secretary, and welfare officers. The ministry is also responsible for organizing training sessions, retreats, and social events for its members.

Joining Criteria

To become an usher, individuals must be fully initiated Catholics and attend the Ushers Ministry meetings. New members undergo an induction process where they are trained in the responsibilities of ushers, including how to handle various situations that may arise during Mass. We welcome new members who are committed to serving the church with dedication and love.

If you are interested in becoming an usher, please contact the Ushers ministry Chairperson or reach out to the parish office.

Activities Involved Within/Beyond the Parish

  • Directing parishioners to their seats during Mass
  • Assisting with collections and offerings during Mass
  • Distributing church bulletins and other materials
  • Attending monthly meetings and training sessions
  • Participating in retreats and workshops organized for ushers
  • Providing assistance at parish events, funerals, and other gatherings
  • Supporting church maintenance and cleanliness before and after Mass
